## Positioning

**The gap in the market:**
Most peptide content is either too clinical (research papers, medical jargon) or too bro-science (gym culture, "get jacked" framing). Nobody is doing **aspirational, design-forward, science-backed peptide education** for the affluent wellness crowd.

**Our positioning:**
> *The most trusted peptide education account for people who take their health seriously.*

Think: the person who reads Andrew Huberman, does morning meditation, travels to Bali, gets IV drips, trains 4x a week. That is the audience. Speak to them at their level.

**Voice:**
- Confident but not arrogant
- Science-backed but human
- Aspirational — this is about living better, longer, sharper
- Never salesy — education builds trust that converts later

## Content Pillars (5)

### 1. THE PROTOCOL
*"What it is, what it does, how to use it"*
One peptide per post. Clean, simple, comprehensive.
Examples:
- BPC-157: The gut & injury healer
- Ipamorelin: Sleep deeper, recover faster
- Epithalon: The longevity peptide
- GHK-Cu: Nature's collagen switch
- Semax: The cognitive edge

### 2. THE SCIENCE
*Mechanism of action, simplified*
Explain the biology without a PhD. Diagrams, analogies, clear language.
Examples:
- How peptides communicate with your cells
- What growth hormone secretagogues actually do
- The difference between oral and injectable peptides
- Why half-life matters

### 3. THE LIFESTYLE
*Peptides in the context of a high-performance life*
Connect peptides to the life your audience is living — travel, training, sleep, stress, skin.
Examples:
- Peptide stack for a long-haul flight
- Recovery protocol after hard training
- The anti-ageing stack for your 40s
- Peptides + cold therapy: what the research says

### 4. THE MYTHS
*Debunking misinformation — builds credibility*
The peptide space is full of bad information. Be the trusted voice that corrects it.
Examples:
- "Peptides are just steroids" — False. Here's why.
- Do you really need to inject? (some you don't)
- The peptide Instagram accounts lying to you
- Why dosing matters more than which peptide you choose

### 5. THE COMMUNITY
*Questions, polls, real experiences*
Stories, Q&As, "what are you running?" polls. Builds engagement and loyalty.

## Content Formats

### Reels (primary growth driver)
- 30-60 seconds
- Hook in first 2 seconds: "This peptide regenerates your gut lining in 4 weeks."
- Text overlay heavy — most watched on mute
- Aesthetic: dark background, clean white/gold text, clinical but beautiful
- Voiceover: AI voice (ElevenLabs) — calm, authoritative, slightly aspirational

### Carousels (saves & shares — best for reach)
- 7-10 slides
- "The complete guide to [peptide]"
- First slide = strong hook visual
- Last slide = CTA: follow, save, share

### Single Image Posts (brand building)
- Minimal design — compound name, clean visual, one key fact
- Works like a quote card but for science
- Highly shareable

### Stories (daily — community building)
- Polls: "Have you tried BPC-157?"
- Q&A boxes weekly
- Behind the scenes of research
- Quick tips and reminders

## Aesthetic Direction

**Colour palette:**
- Background: Deep black `#0A0A0A` or deep navy `#0D1117`
- Primary text: White `#FFFFFF`
- Accent: Gold/amber `#C9A96E` — premium, scientific, warm
- Secondary: Muted green `#4CAF50` — cellular, biological, life

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Bold, clean sans-serif (Syne or Space Grotesk)
- Body: Light, tracked out (Raleway ExtraLight)
- Data/labels: Monospace for credibility

**Visual style:**
- Molecular diagrams simplified to clean line art
- Human body silhouettes with highlighted systems
- Dark editorial photography (ice baths, training, sleep, nature)
- NO cheap stock photos — should feel premium and considered

**Reference accounts for aesthetic inspiration:**
- @hubermanlab (science communication done right)
- @foundmyfitness (Dr. Rhonda Patrick — credibility + depth)
- @dave.asprey (biohacking aspirational lifestyle)

## Legal & Compliance Notes
- **Never make medical claims** — always "research suggests" not "this will"
- **Always recommend consulting a doctor** for injectable protocols
- **Peptides are legal to buy as research chemicals** in most countries — be clear about this
- **Do not name specific suppliers** in early content — builds credibility before monetisation
- **Disclaimer in bio**: "Educational content only. Not medical advice."
