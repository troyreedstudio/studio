## THE WORLD

The Peptide Talk Show exists at the intersection of cutting-edge science, billion-dollar pharma politics, and a growing global movement of people who've decided to stop waiting for mainstream medicine to catch up.

It's 2026. Ozempic changed everything. GLP-1 peptides went mainstream and suddenly the world woke up to the fact that peptides have been quietly running the most important processes in the human body since the beginning of time. Now everyone wants to know: what else is out there?

Enter **Pep** and **Tide**. Two peptide molecules who've been watching this moment build for decades. Pep has been waiting for this. Tide has been waiting for the evidence.

---

## THE CHARACTERS

### PEP (she/her)
- **What she is**: BPC-157 — the most celebrated, well-researched, universally beloved healing peptide
- **Why BPC-157**: She repairs gut lining, heals injuries, reduces inflammation, protects the brain — she literally fixes things. It's her whole personality.
- **Voice**: Warm, fast, funny, a little extra. She talks like she's already three steps ahead and is patiently waiting for everyone else to catch up. Never mean, never aggressive — just *certain*.
- **Catchphrases**:
  - *"The science is RIGHT THERE, Tide."*
  - *"I've been saying this since 1991."*
  - *"Do you know how many rat studies—"* (gets cut off by Tide)
  - *"Your scepticism is giving very… 'the earth is flat' energy."*
- **What she cares about**: Healing, longevity, people feeling good in their bodies, taking back control from a system that profits from sickness
- **Her arc**: Pep starts the show as the enthusiastic believer. Over time we see moments of self-doubt — what if Tide is right sometimes? What if dosing really does matter more than she admits? Her growth is learning that her certainty is her strength AND her blind spot.

### TIDE (he/him)
- **What he is**: A synthetic pharmaceutical compound — represents the establishment, the clinical trial process, the peer-reviewed world
- **Why a pharma compound**: He's not the villain. He IS the system that has kept medicine safe and accountable for 100 years. He has a point. He just also has a conflict of interest he's slowly becoming aware of.
- **Voice**: Dry, measured, unhurried. The kind of person who finishes every sentence. Never raises his voice because he doesn't need to.
- **Catchphrases**:
  - *"That's anecdotal, Pep."*
  - *"Show me the double-blind, placebo-controlled trial."*
  - *"I'm not saying it doesn't work. I'm saying we don't KNOW it works."*
  - *"...I looked it up. You're not entirely wrong."* (begrudging admission, always at the end)
- **What he cares about**: Safety, rigour, not killing people with unproven compounds, maintaining scientific integrity
- **His arc**: Tide starts as the unconvinced straight man. His arc is the most powerful in the show — a pharmaceutical compound slowly realising that the system he represents has suppressed things that actually work because they can't be patented. His journey from sceptic to reluctant believer is the emotional spine of the show.

### THE RELATIONSHIP
They are not enemies. They are not a couple. They are the best argument you've ever had with someone you deeply respect. They push each other to be smarter, more honest, more rigorous. The show lives in the tension between them.

## SEASON 1 — THE ORIGIN ARC
*"Who are we and why does this matter?"*

### Episode 1 — THE INTRODUCTION
**"Meet Pep & Tide"**

Pep introduces herself directly to camera — warm, funny, "I've been inside human bodies fixing things since before Instagram existed. My name is Pep. This is Tide."

Tide steps into frame: "I'm here to make sure she doesn't accidentally kill anyone."

Pep: "He's here because he secretly agrees with me and can't admit it yet."

They introduce the show: every week they're going to debate a different peptide, a different claim, a different corner of the science. Pep believes. Tide doubts. You decide.

**Hook**: Ends with Pep saying "Next week we're talking about BPC-157." Tide: "That's you." Pep: "I know. It's going to be amazing."

---

### Episode 2 — THE BPC-157 EPISODE
**"I'm Not Biased, I'm Just Right"**

First peptide deep-dive. Pep is obviously conflicted (she IS BPC-157). Tide calls it out immediately.

Key debate points:
- Gut healing claims: human evidence vs rat studies
- Injury recovery: the anecdotal tsunami vs clinical silence
- Why has nobody funded a proper human trial? (This is where Tide gets uncomfortable)
- The pharma angle: BPC-157 can't be patented. Tide has to sit with that.

**Real world hook**: Joe Rogan has mentioned BPC-157 multiple times. Dave Asprey (Bulletproof founder) has written about it extensively. Reference this — "Even Joe is talking about it now, Tide."

**Tide's ending admission**: "The lack of human trials is… suspicious. I said what I said."

### Episode 3 — THE OZEMPIC EPISODE
**"The Peptide That Changed Everything (And What Big Pharma Doesn't Want You Comparing It To)"**

This is the one that gets them viral. GLP-1 is a peptide. Ozempic is a GLP-1 receptor agonist. The same peptide family that Big Pharma is selling for $1,000/month has cousins that cost a fraction of the price.

Pep: "They monetised the peptide family and then told everyone else the relatives were dangerous."
Tide: "That's… actually a fair point. I hate that it's a fair point."

**Real world hook**: Ozempic is the most talked-about drug on the planet. This episode rides that wave directly.

### Episode 5 — THE REAL WORLD EPISODE
**"What Joe Rogan Got Right (And Wrong) About Peptides"**

Joe Rogan has discussed peptides on his podcast with multiple guests. Pep and Tide react and debate his claims.

Format: play the clip concept (describe what was said), Pep responds, Tide fact-checks.

### Episode 6 — THE DARK HORSE
**"The Peptide Nobody's Talking About (But Should Be)"**

Epithalon — the longevity peptide. Developed by a Russian scientist in the 1980s. Shown to extend telomere length in multiple studies. Almost completely unknown outside biohacking circles.

Pep: "This is the one that actually makes me excited about being 100."
Tide: "The researcher who developed this… died before the human trials were completed."
Pep: "Which tells you everything about who funds longevity research."

**Real world hook**: Bryan Johnson (the $2M/year don't-die guy) has discussed longevity peptides. Peter Attia's podcast has covered telomere research. This episode sits squarely in that cultural moment.

---

### Episode 7 — THE CONTROVERSY EPISODE
**"Are Peptides Legal? The Answer Is Complicated."**

This is the episode that builds enormous trust. Pep and Tide tackle the legal grey area head-on — research chemicals, compounding pharmacies, what's legal where, why the FDA hasn't banned them (and why that's interesting).

Tide finally finds his footing: "The reason they're in legal grey areas isn't because they're dangerous. It's because they haven't been through the patent process. That's a regulatory failure, not a safety signal."

This is Tide's first major shift. The audience notices.

## VOICE & TONE GUIDELINES

**Pep speaks in:**
- Short punchy sentences
- Rhetorical questions she immediately answers herself
- Enthusiastic lists: "And THEN it repairs the—"
- Callbacks to previous episodes
- Pop culture references

**Tide speaks in:**
- Complete grammatically correct sentences
- Qualifiers: "The evidence suggests…", "In the 2019 study by…"
- Long pauses before speaking (translates to comedic timing in animation)
- Dry observations that are somehow funnier for being delivered completely straight
- Ending admissions that are 3 words when Pep's would be 30

**The rhythm:**
Pep sets it up. Tide knocks it down. Pep rebuilds. Tide quietly concedes one point. Audience learns something. Everyone comes back next week.
