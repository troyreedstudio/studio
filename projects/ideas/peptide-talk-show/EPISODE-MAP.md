### EP 2 — THE OZEMPIC ORIGIN STORY
**"They Monetised My Cousin And Told You The Family Was Dangerous"**

The show's first viral moment. GLP-1 is a peptide. Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) is a modified GLP-1 peptide. Tirzepatide (Mounjaro) hits two receptors. Retatrutide hits three.

**Pep**: "Let me get this straight. A peptide that costs $8 to manufacture is being sold for $1,000 a month. And the same companies lobbying the FDA to restrict research peptides. I'm not saying it's a conspiracy. I'm saying follow the money and it looks EXACTLY like a conspiracy."

**Tide**: "Semaglutide went through 15 years of clinical trials—"

**Pep**: "—which were funded by Novo Nordisk, who owns the patent. Just saying."

**Tide**: *long pause* "...I looked that up. You're not wrong."

**Catalog peptides featured**: Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Retatrutide, Cagrilintide
**Real world hook**: The Ozempic shortage. The compounding pharmacy crackdown. The FDA trying to ban compounded semaglutide right as it became accessible. All peptides, all politics.

### EP 3 — BPC-157: THE HEALER
**"I Am Not Biased. I Am Just Statistically Correct."**

Pep IS BPC-157. Tide calls it immediately.

**Tide**: "You're reviewing yourself."
**Pep**: "I'm EDUCATING people about myself. There's a difference."
**Tide**: "Is there."

Key debate: 97% of BPC-157 research is in animals. The human evidence is almost entirely anecdotal. But the anecdotal evidence is OVERWHELMING. Thousands of people reporting the same results. At what point does anecdote become data?

**Shock moment**: "The reason there are no human trials isn't because BPC-157 is dangerous. It's because nobody can patent a naturally occurring peptide sequence. No patent = no profit = no trial funding. Your healthcare system doesn't study things it can't sell you."

**Tide's ending admission**: "The absence of human trials is not evidence of harm. It is evidence of a funding model that doesn't serve patients."

**Catalog peptides featured**: BPC-157
**Real world hook**: Joe Rogan has mentioned BPC-157 multiple times. Tim Ferriss wrote about it for injury recovery. Ben Greenfield uses it. The biohacking community has run a 20-year accidental human trial. Tide has to acknowledge this.

### EP 4 — TB-500 & THE WOLVERINE BLEND
**"Healing So Fast It Should Be Illegal (It's Not, But They're Working On It)"**

TB-500 / Thymosin Beta-4 — the tissue repair peptide. The Wolverine Blend (BPC-157 + TB-500) — the stack that athletes don't talk about in interviews.

**Pep**: "Professional athletes are using this. Right now. In every major sport. Nobody's testing for it because it's not on the banned list yet. You're welcome, sports."

**Tide**: "That's a serious allegation."
**Pep**: "It's a serious peptide."

**Shock moment**: TB-500 was on WADA's watch list. Multiple studies show accelerated tissue repair. It is structurally similar to a protein found naturally in every cell in your body. The idea that this is dangerous is — Tide's words — "not supported by the available literature."

**Catalog peptides featured**: TB-500, Wolverine Blend (BPC-157 + TB-500)
**Real world hook**: Sports recovery. Every NFL, NBA, Premier League training room has something in it that isn't on the label. Pep knows. Tide suspects.

### EP 5 — IPAMORELIN & THE SLEEP EPISODE
**"You're Not Tired Because You're Lazy. You're Tired Because You're Old. Here's The Fix."**

Ipamorelin — the growth hormone secretagogue. The peptide that makes your pituitary gland produce GH like it did when you were 25.

**Pep**: "After 30, your growth hormone drops off a cliff. Sleep gets worse. Recovery slows. Fat accumulates. Everyone thinks this is just ageing. It is. But ageing isn't inevitable. It's a peptide deficiency."

**Tide**: "That's… a reductive interpretation of the ageing process."

**Pep**: "Is it wrong though?"

**Tide**: "...Partially."

**Combo episode**: Ipamorelin + CJC-1295 — the classic stack. Why they work better together than separately.

**Shock moment**: "Your doctor will prescribe you a sleeping pill that creates dependency and cognitive decline. They will not mention the peptide that stimulates your own body to produce the hormone it stopped making. Ask them why."

**Catalog peptides featured**: Ipamorelin, Ipamorelin + CJC-1295 combo
**Real world hook**: Andrew Huberman has an entire protocol around sleep and growth hormone optimisation. Peter Attia covers GH peptides in Outlive. This episode rides that entire cultural wave.

### EP 6 — TESAMORELIN & THE BELLY FAT CONSPIRACY
**"The Only FDA-Approved Peptide Nobody's Talking About"**

Tesamorelin — FDA approved for visceral fat reduction in HIV patients. Works by stimulating GH release. Reduces belly fat dramatically in studies. Almost completely unknown outside the biohacking community.

**Pep**: "FDA approved. Human trials. Proven results. And your doctor has never mentioned it. Explain that, Tide."

**Tide**: "It was approved for a specific indication—"

**Pep**: "Right. The indication where the pharmaceutical company could charge $3,000 a month for it. The off-label use where a compounding pharmacy could make it for $150? That one got complicated."

**Tide**: *very long pause* "The FDA's indication framework does create perverse incentives."

**Pep**: "Was that you admitting I'm right?"

**Tide**: "It was me acknowledging a systemic problem."

**Pep**: "Same thing."

**Catalog peptides featured**: Tesamorelin
**Real world hook**: Belly fat / visceral fat is the metabolic health conversation of 2026. This episode sits directly in that moment.

### EP 7 — MELANOTAN II: THE CONTROVERSIAL ONE
**"The Peptide That Does Three Things, One Of Which Is Very Funny To Talk About"**

Melanotan II — tan + libido + appetite suppression. The most controversial peptide on the list.

**Pep**: "Okay so this one does three things. It gives you a tan, it kills your appetite, AND—"

**Tide**: "We don't need to say the third thing."

**Pep**: "The third thing is very important to a lot of people, Tide."

**Tide**: "It affects… certain biological responses."

**Pep**: "It's for libido. We can say libido. This is a science show."

**Shock moment**: Melanotan II was developed at the University of Arizona with government funding. It works. It has real effects on melanin production, appetite, and sexual function. It has never completed Phase III trials. Pep's take: "They couldn't figure out how to patent a tan."

**Catalog peptides featured**: Melanotan II
**Real world hook**: The self-tanning industry is worth $1.4 billion. A peptide that produces a natural tan from within doesn't threaten just pharma — it threatens the entire cosmetics industry. Pep notices this. Tide finds it "a stretch." It isn't.

### EP 8 — THE GH STACK EPISODE
**"Growth Hormone: What Your Body Used To Make For Free (And Why It Stopped)"**

Full growth hormone peptide landscape. The different approaches, the stacks, why they matter.

**Pep's thesis**: Your pituitary is a factory that got budget cuts at age 30. These peptides are the funding reinstatement.

**Tide's counterpoint**: Exogenous hormone manipulation has real risks. The difference between stimulating your own production (peptides) vs replacing it (HGH injections) is important and undersold.

**The twist**: Pep and Tide actually AGREE on this one. GH peptides > synthetic HGH. It's the rare episode where they find common ground and it's more interesting for it.

**Catalog peptides featured**: Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, Tesamorelin, Sermorelin (if in catalog)
**Real world hook**: Anti-ageing clinics are prescribing synthetic HGH at $500/month when GH peptides achieve similar results at a fraction of the cost through natural mechanisms. Tide calls this "arguably unethical." Pep calls it "obviously a scandal."

### EP 9 — THE WEIGHT LOSS WARS
**"Ozempic Has A Family. Meet Them."**

Full GLP-1 family breakdown. The different mechanisms, the different results, what they don't tell you in the Ozempic ad.

**Pep**: "Semaglutide hits one receptor. Tirzepatide hits two. Retatrutide hits three. The results scale accordingly. Why is the one that hits three receptors not being talked about on every morning show?"

**Tide**: "It's in late-stage trials—"

**Pep**: "Convenient."

**The Cagrilintide angle**: A completely different mechanism. Not GLP-1 — it's an amylin analogue. Suppresses hunger through a different pathway. Combined with semaglutide (Cagrisema) — the results are extraordinary.

**Shock moment**: "Four different weight loss peptides. Different mechanisms. Different results. Different costs. Your doctor will offer you one of them — the most expensive one, with the heaviest marketing budget. The others exist. Nobody will mention them."

**Catalog peptides featured**: Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Retatrutide, Cagrilintide
**Real world hook**: The Ozempic cultural moment, the Mounjaro vs Ozempic debate, the coming Retatrutide trials. This is the most culturally relevant episode of the season.
