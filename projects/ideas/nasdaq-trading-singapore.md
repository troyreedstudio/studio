## 💰 Tax — The Full Picture

### What MAS Regulated Means
MAS = Monetary Authority of Singapore (central bank + financial regulator). If a broker is MAS-regulated:
- Money held under Singapore's regulatory framework
- Broker must meet capital requirements
- Legal recourse if something goes wrong
- Non-MAS brokers = money overseas with fewer protections

### Capital Gains: ZERO TAX ✅
Singapore is one of the best places in the world to trade US stocks:
1. **US doesn't tax foreign investors on capital gains** (non-resident aliens exempt)
2. **Singapore has NO capital gains tax**
3. Buy at $10, sell at $100 → keep all $90. Legally. Zero tax.
4. Must file **W-8BEN form** when opening account (declares you're not a US person)

### Dividends: 30% Withholding Tax ❌
- US takes 30% of dividends before they reach you
- Singapore has NO tax treaty with US to reduce this
- Example: $100 dividend → you receive $70

### 🔑 Tax Workarounds (Legal)

**1. Focus on growth stocks (no/low dividends)**
TSLA, NVDA, AMZN, META, GOOG pay zero or tiny dividends. Returns come from price growth = 100% tax-free.

**2. Use Ireland-domiciled ETFs (the smart hack)**
Instead of US-listed ETFs, buy Irish equivalents on London Stock Exchange:

| Instead of... | Buy... | Dividend tax |
|---|---|---|
| VOO (S&P 500) | CSPX (iShares, Ireland) | **15%** instead of 30% |
| QQQ (Nasdaq) | CNDX (iShares, Ireland) | **15%** instead of 30% |
| VTI (Total US) | VUSA (Vanguard, Ireland) | **15%** instead of 30% |

Ireland has US tax treaty: 30% → 15%. Ireland doesn't charge Singapore residents. Half the tax.

**3. Accumulating ETFs (reinvest dividends automatically)**
Some Irish ETFs are "accumulating" — dividends automatically reinvested, never paid out to you. No withholding event at all.

**4. US Estate Tax avoidance**
- US holdings >US$60,000 at death: US can tax up to 40%
- Irish-domiciled ETFs avoid this entirely (not US-situs assets)

### Tax Summary Table

| What | Tax |
|---|---|
| Capital gains on US stocks | **0%** ✅ |
| Dividends on growth stocks (TSLA, NVDA, AMZN) | **~0%** ✅ |
| Dividends via Irish ETFs (CSPX, CNDX) | **15%** ✅ |
| Dividends on US-listed ETFs (VOO, QQQ) | **30%** ❌ |
| Singapore income tax on trading profits | **0%** if personal investment ✅ |

### ⚠️ Caveat
If IRAS considers you a professional trader (trading = main income, high frequency, short holds), they could classify gains as income. As long as you're investing alongside your Amazon job, you're fine.
